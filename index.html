I am the Sewell Family–McAllister Distinguished Professor of Medicine in the Division of Cardiology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where I serve as Director of Cardiac Electrophysiology and Program Director of the Clinical Cardiac Electrophysiology Fellowship.

My career bridges two worlds: the precision of electrical engineering — where I began at MIT — and the art of medicine. Cardiac electrophysiology became the natural synthesis: cool technology, a problem-solving mentality, and the fulfillment of doing something curative for patients. My research focuses on improving care for patients with atrial fibrillation, from novel ablation techniques to system-wide care pathway redesign and digital health.

Active Funded Projects
REACT-AF Trial: Rhythm Evaluation for Anticoagulation with Continuous Monitoring
2023 – Present · Principal Investigator · PCORI
Active

REACT-AF is a PCORI-funded trial evaluating whether continuous atrial fibrillation monitoring to guide anticoagulation decisions can reduce stroke and bleeding compared to continuous therapy. The trial addresses a fundamental clinical question: can rhythm-guided anticoagulation be safely implemented with modern monitoring technologies?

Updates

2024Enrollment ongoing. Multi-site coordination underway.
Left vs. Left: CRT with His/Left Bundle Branch Pacing vs. Biventricular Pacing (RCT)
2024 – Present · Principal Investigator · PCORI
Active

A randomized clinical trial comparing cardiac resynchronization therapy using His/left bundle branch area pacing versus biventricular pacing with a left ventricular epicardial lead in patients with heart failure (LVEF ≤50%) and wide QRS or anticipated high pacing burden.

Improving Outpatient AF Care Across Central NC: Direct Primary Care & Patient Engagement
2021 – Present · Principal Investigator · Bristol Myers Squibb Foundation · $1,169,615

A comprehensive program to improve atrial fibrillation care for underserved populations across central North Carolina by engaging primary care providers and patients through a novel care network, tele-mentoring, and digital health tools.

OCEANIC-AF: Asundexian vs. Apixaban in Atrial Fibrillation
2022 – Present · Principal Investigator · Bayer Pharmaceuticals
Active

A Phase III industry-sponsored trial evaluating the novel Factor XIa inhibitor asundexian compared to apixaban for stroke prevention in atrial fibrillation. UNC is a participating clinical site.

Program Overview

The Clinical Cardiac Electrophysiology (CCEP) Fellowship at UNC provides comprehensive training in the evaluation and management of the full spectrum of cardiac arrhythmia conditions. Training is structured around ACGME core competencies and the HRS Clinical Competency Statement for fellowship training.

Fellows receive hands-on training in: catheter ablation for SVT, VT, atrial flutter, and atrial fibrillation (including epicardial approaches); pacemaker and ICD implantation; laser lead extraction; left atrial appendage occlusion; and EP study interpretation. The state-of-the-art facilities at UNC Medical Center provide fellows access to advanced technologies and high procedural volumes.

The educational program includes daily patient-care centered discussions, weekly didactic and electrogram conferences, journal club, and structured research mentorship. Dr. Gehi mentors fellows personally in research, with a strong track record of fellows going on to academic and clinical leadership positions.

Clinical Training Areas
Ablation Procedures Catheter ablation for paroxysmal and persistent AF · Hybrid epicardial–endocardial AF ablation · SVT ablation (AVNRT, AVRT, AT) · Ventricular tachycardia ablation · Atrial flutter ablation
Device Therapy Pacemaker implantation (single, dual, biventricular) · ICD implantation · Subcutaneous ICD · Conduction system pacing (His/LBBP) · Laser lead extraction · WATCHMAN LAA occlusion
Diagnostics & Mapping 3D electroanatomic mapping (CARTO, EnSite) · EP study and induction protocols · Esophageal temperature monitoring · Remote cardiac monitoring interpretation
Research & Scholarship Fellows are expected to complete at least one original research project resulting in a publication or abstract presentation. Protected time for research is available.
