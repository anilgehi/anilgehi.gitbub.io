I am the Sewell Family–McAllister Distinguished Professor of Medicine in the Division of Cardiology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where I serve as Director of Cardiac Electrophysiology and Program Director of the Clinical Cardiac Electrophysiology Fellowship.

My career bridges two worlds: the precision of electrical engineering — where I began at MIT — and the art of medicine. Cardiac electrophysiology became the natural synthesis: cool technology, a problem-solving mentality, and the fulfillment of doing something curative for patients. My research focuses on improving care for patients with atrial fibrillation, from novel ablation techniques to system-wide care pathway redesign and digital health.

UNC Clinical Cardiac Electrophysiology Fellowship
A 24-month ACGME-accredited training program in all aspects of cardiac electrophysiology. Dr. Gehi has served as Program Director since 2012, leading one of the primary referral centers for complex arrhythmias in the southeastern United States.

Program Overview
Catheter ablation procedure, UNC EP laboratory
Atrial fibrillation ECG — characteristic irregular rhythm

The Clinical Cardiac Electrophysiology Fellowship at UNC provides comprehensive training in evaluation and management of the full spectrum of cardiac arrhythmia conditions. Training is structured around ACGME core competencies and the HRS Clinical Competency Statement.

Fellows receive hands-on training in catheter ablation (SVT, VT, AF, atrial flutter, epicardial approaches), pacemaker and ICD implantation, laser lead extraction, left atrial appendage occlusion, and EP study interpretation. The state-of-the-art UNC facilities and high procedural volume provide an outstanding training environment.

TextbookCorrelative Anatomy for the Electrophysiologist – Asirvatham *
Discusses relevant anatomic structures for common ablations with excellent relative anatomy focus. Dr. Syed authored some chapters during his time at Mayo.

TextbookAnatomy for Cardiac Electrophysiologists – Ho/Ernst
Good introductory anatomy book. If choosing between this and the Atlas, choose the Atlas.

Fantastic anatomic atlas using the McAlpine dissections from UCLA. Open access and downloadable for free. A 2nd volume focusing on ablation is in progress.

TextbookPractical Clinical Electrophysiology – Zimetbaum/Josephson
Good high-level introductory EP book. Discusses basic EP study mechanics and ablation strategies.
📘
TextbookHandbook of Cardiac Electrophysiology – Murgatroyd *
A bit dated but ages well. Covers lab setup and basic catheters used in EP.
📘
TextbookCardiac Electrophysiology – Purves
Complementary to Murgatroyd. Lowest priority of the three basics.
